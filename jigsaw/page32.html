Japanese puzzles that can drive you
crazy, "pure white hell" and "dark hell"

To what extent can a person abuse himself? What kind of motivation was used to invent such a jigsaw puzzle? These are unknown, but at the first glance at this "pure hell", I believe you will never have the urge to play it. This extremely challenging jigsaw puzzle game was created by a sadomasochistic designer from Beverly, Japan. It has a 1000-piece version and a 2000-piece version.

All puzzles are made of a solid color template, which is cut into countless small pieces. As long as they are scattered, you will feel that all the puzzle pieces are exactly the same, and there is no sense of contradiction where they are placed. But the pain is that all you see are cruel illusions.

The puzzle is actually composed of seven different shapes of small pieces, and each piece has its proper position. Although it seems that any two pieces can be spliced together, if you find that it is more laborious when splicing, or there is an obvious dislocation, it means you made a mistake.

A mistake in one place may cause a wide range of dislocations, and you often cannot find them in time. After you really feel that something is wrong, you may have to overthrow it all and start over. And the feeling of falling into hell is the inspiration for the name of this game.
